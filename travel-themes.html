Russia's Wild Frontiers

From Arctic tundra to volcanic peaks — handpicked destinations across Russia.

Kamchatka Peninsula

A world-class wild frontier famed for active volcanoes, boiling geysers, hot springs, and brown bear tracking. The ultimate playground for heli-skiing, volcano trekking, and river rafting.

Lake Baikal

The world's deepest and oldest freshwater lake. Offers dramatic summer trekking and kayaking, alongside extreme winter adventures like ice diving, hovercraft racing, and driving across transparent black ice.

Altai Republic

Known as the "Russian Tibet," this region features snow-capped peaks, rushing white-water rivers, and vast pine forests — favoured for multi-day horseback expeditions, mountaineering, and demanding river rafting.

Mount Elbrus

The highest peak in Europe (5,642 m) in the Caucasus Mountains. A major mountaineering magnet for high-altitude trekking, alpine climbing, and extreme backcountry snowboarding.

Murmansk & Kola Peninsula

Above the Arctic Circle, this is the prime zone for winter expeditions — snowmobile safaris across the tundra, dog sledding, and hunting the Northern Lights.

Lake Baikal

The oldest, deepest, and clearest freshwater lake on Earth. Protected by the Pribaikalsky National Park. Famous for summer trekking and mesmerizing winter ice formations.

Kamchatka Peninsula

A dramatic Pacific ring-of-fire landscape packed with active volcanoes, geysers, thermal hot springs, and a dense population of wild brown bears.

Altai Mountains

Known as the "Russian Tibet", offering pure air, alpine meadows, fast-flowing glacial rivers, and dramatic snow-capped peaks perfect for rafting and trekking.

Russian Karelia

A northern paradise filled with dense pine forests, pristine lakes, and rocky fjords bordering Finland. Features the wild Paanajärvi National Park.

Moscow & St. Petersburg

The most popular option for first-time family travel, offering a seamless mix of educational monuments, parks, and world-class kid-friendly attractions.

Sochi & The Black Sea Coast

Russia's premier resort city presents a subtropical climate, sandy beaches, high-tech amusement parks, and accessible mountain activities.

Karelia — The Land of Lakes

Located in northwest Russia near Finland, Karelia is a nature lover's paradise ideal for families seeking fresh air, outdoor recreation, and fairytale landscapes.

The Imperial Classics (Moscow & St. Petersburg)

Best for culture, art, shopping, and first-time travelers.

The Chasing Auroras Tour (Arctic & St. Petersburg)

Best for winter wonderlands, photography, and active group adventures.
